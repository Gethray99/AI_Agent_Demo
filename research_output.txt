The stock market, also known as the equity market or share market, is a collection of markets and exchanges where the issuing and trading of publicly held companies' shares of stock take place. It is a marketplace where investors can buy and sell ownership stakes in publicly traded companies. The stock market plays a crucial role in the economy by facilitating capital formation, enabling companies to raise funds for expansion and investment, and providing investors with opportunities for wealth creation. Prices in the stock market are determined by supply and demand, with factors such as company performance, economic conditions, and investor sentiment influencing fluctuations. There are various types of stock markets, including primary markets where new securities are issued, and secondary markets where existing securities are traded. Major stock markets around the world include the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE), Nasdaq, London Stock Exchange (LSE), and Tokyo Stock Exchange (TSE). Understanding the stock market is essential for investors seeking to participate in the financial markets.
